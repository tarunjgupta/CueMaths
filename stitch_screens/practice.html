The Stokes' Theorem Application

Consider the vector field F(x, y, z) = (y - z)i + (z - x)j + (x - y)k. Calculate the circulation of F around the triangle C with vertices (1,0,0), (0,1,0), and (0,0,1).

Requirements: Express the solution via the surface integral of the curl of F over the planar region S bounded by C.

Structural Inconsistency

Your determinant expansion for the k component is correct, but your notation for partial derivatives lacks formal rigour. Use ∂/∂x(z-x) for clarity.

Contextual Insight

Observe the symmetry in F. The vector field is purely rotational. The resulting constant curl vector (-2, -2, -2) is a hallmark of linear rotational fields.

description Historical Annotation

Stokes' Theorem links the microscopic rotation of a field (curl) to the macroscopic flow along a boundary. This specific problem was first noted in 19th-century hydrodynamics journals.
